Session 1

This page is part of the CU CSFP Arduino Project.

In this session we will be building a small "Smoke Detector" appliance. We will program the Arduino to detect smoke and sound an alarm. We will also explore adding to this functionally with things like rest/test buttons, LEDs, heat sensors, etc. Although we will not go that far, the pcDuino could be sued to enhance our Smoke Detector to build an Internet connect "Smart" smoke detector similar to the Nest Protect.

Note: Although we will be building a basic "smoke detector", our smoke detector should not be used in any life-critical application (i.e. as a replacement for a REAL smoke detector). Commercial smoke detectors go through a variety of safety certifications and have been carefully designed to ensure that they will function correctly in a range of circumstance and that they will help save your life in the event of a fire. Our device should not be mistaken for or used as a life-saving device. It is not one.
